1. Recognizing Serials

Give as many reasons as you can (there may be more than one per example) why the following titles could or could not be cataloged as serials.

Example 1

Page [iii]:

 

Roger

Ebert's

Movie

Yearbook

1999

Page iv:

Copyright © 1996, 1997, 1998 Roger Ebert

ISBN 0-8362-6831-8

Page 4 of cover:

Andrew McMeel Publishing

 

1. Issued frequently (apparently annually) so could be cataloged as a serial.

 

Example 2

Page [1]:

33RD PUBLICATION DESIGN ANNUAL

ROCKPORT PUBLISHERS

Page [2]:

c. 1998 The Society of Publication Designers, Inc.

First published in the United States of America by:

Rockport Publishers, Inc

33 Commercial Street

Gloucester, Massachusetts 01930

ISBN 1-56496-501-5

1. Title includes a statement of frequency.

2. It bears numbering that is a designation (33rd).

ISBN 0-8129-1892-4

4.New Records

Example 1

1st issue in hand t.p.:

STATISTIQUES DE

RECETTES PUBLIQUES

DES PAYS MEMBRES DE LOCDE

REVENUE STATISTICS

OF OECD MEMBER COUNTRIES

1965-1984

 

 

2nd issue in hand t.p.:

REVENUE STATISTICS

OF OECD MEMBER COUNTRIES

1965-1985

 

 

3rd issue in hand t.p.:

REVENUE STATISTICS

OF OECD MEMBER COUNTRIES

STATISTIQUES DE

RECETTES PUBLIQUES

DES PAYS MEMBRES DE LOCDE

1965-1987

New record? (circle one): Yes

Why?

 The French title was chosen as the title proper. It disappears with the 1965-1985 issue and that requires a new record. No new record is needed, however, for the third issue since it represents only a change in the order of parallel titles.

 

Example 2.

1st issue in hand t.p.:

The Steel Market

In 1992

 

 

2nd issue in hand t.p.:

The Steel Market

In 1993

and

Prospects

For 1994

 

 

3rd issue in hand t.p.:

The Steel Market

In 1994

and

Prospects

For 1995

New record? (circle one): Yes

Why?

 There is a change within the first five words for the 1994 issue, so a new record is required for it.

 

Example 3.

1st issue in hand t.p.:

COUNTRY PROFILE

Costa Rica

Panama

1995-96

 

 

2nd issue in hand t.p.:

COUNTRY PROFILE

Costa Rica

1998-99

New record? (circle one): Yes

Why?

 "Costa Rica" disappears from the title with the 1998-99 issue and that is a change within the first five words as well as a change in scope for the journal.
