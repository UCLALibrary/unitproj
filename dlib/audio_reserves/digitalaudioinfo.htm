UCLA Music Library
Digital Audio Reserves

Online listening assignments are provided as a service to UCLA Music faculty and students. The listening examples are drawn from the UCLA Music Library collections, and if necessary, from the collections of the instructor. Listening assignments are made available only for the duration of the class for which they are intended, and access is limited to the students enrolled in the class.

The Music Library adheres to the UCLA Library copyright policies, and in particular to policies regarding Electronic Reserve operations. Music Library policies and practices have also been informed by the Music Library Association guidelines on the digital transmission of electronic reserves.

The files are streamed to the user's workstation on demand. Most of the files provided are in the RealMedia format (real.com), but Quicktime is used for some classes. In order to play the musical examples users must have the RealPlayer and Quicktime clients (as appropriate) on their workstation.

Faculty should submit requests for the creation of online listening assignments well in advance of each quarter. A form for making the request is available from the Reserves Assistant. The form can also be downloaded (Word 97 format) or submitted via the web.

The RealMedia files are encoded for two different streaming rates: 16kbps and 82kbps. The lower rate allows access via a modem; the higher rate is appropriate for broadband connections. The Real server automatically provides the appropriate stream depending on the bandwidth available (bandwidth negotiation).
