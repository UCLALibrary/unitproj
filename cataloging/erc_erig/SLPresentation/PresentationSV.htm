PEW STUDY

Of the 53% of U.S. adults who said they visited libraries in 2007, the biggest users were young adults aged 18 to 30 in the tech-loving group known as Generation Y, the survey by the Pew Internet & American Life Project said.

Internet users were more than twice as likely to patronize libraries as non-Internet users, according to the survey.

More than two-thirds of library visitors in all age groups said they used computers while at the library.

The survey showed 62% of Generation Y respondents said they visited a public library in the past year, with a steady decline in usage according to age. Some 57% of adults aged 43 to 52 said they visited a library in 2007, followed by 46% of adults aged 53 to 61, 42% of adults aged 62 to 71, and just 32% of adults over 72.

Public libraries now offer virtual homework help and special gaming software programs, and some librarians even have created characters in the Second Life virtual world.

WHAT IS SECOND LIFE?

 

Second Life (SL) is a 3D virtual world and is an example of social computing. Unlike other 3D worlds, all of what is in SL is built by the users. There is no pre-existing back-story.

 

 

Areas within the virtual world are divided into islands aka SIMs. SIMs are the computer servers on which all the programming for an island is stored. A group of SIMs is a grid.

 

You can explore and participate in SL for free. If you want to build something in SL, you will have to purchase or rent real estate, which will require you to subscribe to SL for a monthly fee or pay a fee for a rental to a property owner.

NMC VIRTUAL WORLDS

 

2007 was a year in which campuses made their entries into Second Life in great numbers, and as a result there is a considerable critical mass of educational institutions now existent in Second Life more than 1200 educational islands were created in 2007, and the number of educational projects launched last year in Second Life is many times that.

 

2008 also promises to see strong educational use of Second Life, but with a new focus. Projects in 2008 are much more likely to be about creating learning experiences than virtual campuses.

 

New initiatives for 2008 include:

 

  The NMC Virtual Learning Prize. The NMC Virtual Worlds team will be soliciting ideas for the development of immersive content from educators around the globe. Twenty ideas will be selected in 2008 for development. Each Virtual Learning prize recipient will receive $5,000 US in cash incentives and virtual services that can be devoted to realizing the idea in Second Life. Recipients will have their idea funded with $4,500 US in premium development services from NMC and receive an honorarium of $500 US in cash. The copyright for prize winning ideas will of course remain with the inventor, but the products created as part of this program will go into open source and be distributed royalty free to the educational community. This program will be launched in March, 2008.

 

  The Educational Resource Repository. The NMC is devoting an entire sim for an educational resources repository that will be located on Learning, an island within the NMC Campus. To seed the content of this repository, and to encourage other developers to support education in Second Life, the NMC will release virtually all the content it produced in 2007 into open source buildings, meeting facilities, educational tools, avatars, clothing, equipment, scripts, and much more. The amount of material that the NMC has licensed to release in this way numbers in the thousands of objects, and the NMC is committed to adding to it throughout the year. The repository, in which everything from shoes to amphitheaters will be available for a single Linden dollar (simply to enable tracking via counts), will also include resources donated by other developers. The first of these is Stephane Zugzwangs renowned Virtual Reality Room, which is already available. The NMCs Educational Resources Repository will open February 1, 2008

 

  Development of New Forms of Architecture. Among the interests of the NMC Virtual Worlds team for 2008 is to explore new concepts of architecture that may be especially suited for virtual spaces. The first of these, created by real-life architect and NMC Virtual Worlds developer CJ Holden, is an extremely flexible modular structure that works with the SL building grid to allow the construction of simple or complex structures organically in literally minutes.

 

  Live mentoring for new entrants into Second Life. High on the list of planned enhancements for the NMCs Orientation Island experience is the implementation of a mentoring program. There is strong support for this in the Second Life educational community, and a presence-aware mentor notification system has already been piloted. The mentor community is expected to launch sometime in the first part of 2008.

THE SALAMANDER PROJECT

 

An area of concern for librarians in SL is how to organize the educational material in SL. One approach, The Salamander Project, is a collaborative project coordinated by researchers and educators at The Center for Advanced Technology in Education at the University of Oregon.

 

The project mission is to enlist people to collaborate to find, index, and discuss aspects of the 3D Virtual World Second Life that has educational value and share in the research, development, and training opportunities associated with those factors.

 

 

The Salamander Project has a "Proof of Concept" grant from the NorthWest Academic Computing Consortium for $10,000. With this startup money, the following objectives are intended to be accomplished:

  Create several tools for nominating Learning Objects within Second Life.

 

  Establish a working taxonomy for these Learning Objects within Second Life on the SaLamander wiki.

 

  Develop some initial capacity for Second Life Learning Objects to be found within MERLOT: http://www.merlot.org/merlot/index.htm.

MERLOT is a user-centered, searchable collection of peer reviewed, higher education, online learning materials created by registered members, and a set of faculty development support services.

 

Do check out the SaLamander wiki. There is much information regarding educational materials, tools, and management systems in development for SL on this wiki: http://www.eduisland.net/salamanderwiki/index.php?title=Main_Page#Participant_Registration_and_Contact_Information

A video overview of SaLamander is available: http://blip.tv/file/571587

Wainbrave Bernal (SL avatar name) gave a presentation on SaLamander. The presentation slides are available here: http://sl.nmc.org/wp-content/uploads/2008/01/nmc-salamander.pdf

REGISTER

 

To register for Second Life, visit the SL website: http://secondlife.com/

 

You will have to choose a name in order to register. You will be provided with a list of last names from which you must choose a last name. You will also have to choose a first name of your choosing.

 

 

Download the SL software onto your computer.

ORIENTATION ISLAND

 

Once you have registered and logged onto SL, you will be given several avatar forms from which to choose. You may have to wait a few moments for your avatar to be born. Your name will appear above your avatar. You will find yourself at the entrance to Orientation Island.

 

Be careful when you begin. Once you leave Orientation Island, you may not go back to it.

 

Orientation Island has four modules which you can work your way through. They will teach you the basics of SL, including navigating around SL and editing your appearance.

MOVING YOUR AVATAR IN SL

 

To move your avatar, use the up, down, and side arrows. To fly up use the page up key; use the page down key to come down.

APPEARANCE

 

You are given a choice of several avatar types when you first register and log onto SL. Not all of them are human forms. Once you have chosen your basic type, you can make changes to your appearance.

 

You can change your hair, choose your eyes, and alter your size (both vertically and horizontally). You can also change the default set of clothing you are given.

 

Once you get familiar with navigating and making basic changes to your appearance, you can go shopping to buy attachments to alter your appearance. You can, for example, buy prim hair which is worn as an attachment. You can also buy skin with anatomical parts. Check out some videos on this before you buy, so you know how to choose well made attachments. You dont what seams to show or have a George Hamilton sun tan.

 

Shopping is VERY big in SL, and you can find stores to buy your avatar all sorts of clothes, to suit ANY occasion, and probably any time period as well. You can often find freebies, (free clothes, accessories, houses, modes of transportation, plants, drinks, food, etc.) as well.

 

See the Second Life Tutorial on how to change your appearance below to get you started. How to change your appearance: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GqbuMi5N97c&feature=related

RIGHT CLICKING IS IMPORTANT

 

Right clicking your mouse allows your avatar to perform many common activities in SL. What those activities are depends upon on what you click. Right clicking opens up a pie divided into eight segments with the bottom segment giving you the option to display another pie. The labels representing the activities your avatar can perform which are displayed if you click on an object will be different than those displayed if you click on another avatar.

 

 

When you click on an object, the first set of options you will see is:

 

  Sit Here

  Touch

  Create

  Edit

  More>

HOW TO SIT

Navigate your avatar so that it is in the vicinity in front of the object on which you want to sit.

  Right click on the object.

 

  Click on Sit Here.

 

 

To stand up, click on the Stand Up tab at the bottom center of your screen.

 

 

Be sure to position yourself in front of the object on which you want to sit, otherwise you may find yourself sitting in direction you do not want to face.

TOUCH

 

Touching an object may allow you to animate, teleport, access a notecard, or direct you to a website. To touch an object, right click your mouse, and left click touch.

 

MORE>

Clicking on more will give you additional options such as Wear to change your clothing.

 

Clicking on More> again will bring up options which will allow you to Report Abuse or to Inspect which provides you with information about an object, such as who created it.

COMMUNICATING IN SL

There are three ways to communicate in SL.

CHAT

 

  Chatting is used to communicate to other avatars by using your computer keyboard to type in the chat field. This is the most common form of communication in SL.

 

  To chat, click on the Chat tab located on the lower left side of your computer screen.

 

  A chat field will open. Click inside the chat field and type your message. Then press Enter on your key board to send your chat.

 

  The message you sent will pop up for several seconds on your screen above the chat field. When someone in your vicinity sends a chat message, you will see it pop up here.

 

There are times when you may be with a group where several people are chatting simultaneously and several conversational threads are happening at once. To ease following the threads, click on the History button to the left of chat field. A window will stay open until you close it which will contain all the messages sent by people in the vicinity. You will be able to scroll up and down this window and read the messages.

 

  Instant messaging is used when you want your communication to be private, when sending a message to a group, when communicating with someone in your Friends list who is online in a location different from that where you are, and when leaving a message for someone who is not online.

 

 

INSTANT MESSAGE

 

You may want to communicate privately with an avatar so that others around you cannot hear (or see) what you are saying. To do this, right click on the avatar with whom you wish to communicate, and left click on Send IM. A window will open and you can type your message in the chat field at the bottom of the window. Click on send to send the message to the other avatar. Your chat session will appear in the window about the chat field. When the avatar responds to you, you will see an indication in the window that the avatar is typing their response to you.

 

You can also leave an Instant Message for another avatar who is online, but in a different location from you or who is not online. You can do this by highlighting the name of the avatar if you have them in your friends list.

 

  Click on the Communicate tab.

 

  Click on Contacts.

 

  Click on the Friends tab.

 

  Click on the name of the avatar to whom you wish to send a message.

 

  Click on IM/Call and type your message. If the person is in SL they will respond. If not, your message will be delivered to them when they log onto SL.

 

VOICE

 

  Voice is used when you wish to talk to someone within voice distance. You will need to have a microphone and speakers or headsets to use this function. You will also have to set your voice preferences by clicking on Edit, then Preferences, then voice.

 

SETTING PREFERENCES

 

You will need to set preferences for how you want your computer to handle things such as sound and video streaming, voice, etc. To set preferences, click on Edit, then click on Preferences at the bottom of the popup menu. A window with a list of buttons on the left will open. Click on the button for the settings you want to adjust.

 

CURRENCY

 

You will want to register without signing up for a subscription. However, you may want to give credit card information. When I signed on, I was given L$250 for entering my credit card information.

 

You can participate in SL without using real money or a credit card. SL has its own currency, Linden dollars (L$). You may purchase L$s by buying them with real money, you can try and find a job in SL and earn some, or you can build things in SL and sell them for L$s in SL. SEARCHING IN SL

 

To locate library-related groups in SL, click on the Search tab located at the bottom your computer screen. It is located in the middle of the screen. You can search for events, places, people, and groups. When searching for places, be sure you have the mature box checked.

 

LOCATING LIBRARY GROUPS

 

To locate library-related groups in SL, click on the Search tab located at the bottom your computer screen. It is located in the middle of the screen. When the search screen opens, click on the Groups tab at the top of the screen. It is the last tab. Be sure the Include Mature groups box is checked. Type in librarians in the Find field and click on search. A list of librarian groups will appear.

 

In addition, there are numerous library committees which you may join once you are a member of the Librarians of Second Life group.

 

There are 797 members in the Librarians of Second Life group.

Check out their website, Second Life L!brary Staff Worksite: http://www.infoisland.org/drupal/

 

INVENTORY

 

Animations, scripts, notes, clothes, body parts, landmarks, objects, gestures, and folders are stored in your inventory. Your avatar will come with a Library in your inventory that has a basic set of some of these features. You access your inventory by clicking on the Inventory tab at the bottom right of your screen.

 

 

You can create new folders, notes, etc., by clicking on Create in the menu at the top of your Inventory.

 

You can share landmarks, notes, folders, etc. with other avatars by dragging an item from your Inventory to the avatar with whom you want to share the item.

 

You can send something to an avatar that is not online. If they are in your Friends List, highlight their name, and open up their profile. Then drag the item from your Inventory to their profile.

 

If they are not in your Friends List, access the Search function by clicking on the Search tab at the bottom of your screen. In the Search window, click on the People tab and enter the name of the avatar in the search field. Open the avatars profile and drag the item from your Inventory onto the avatars profile.

BUILDING IN SL

 

The basic building blocks in SL are called prims (short for primitives). Since storage capacity on servers has limitations, the size of the servers impacts the number of prims allowed on a server. Everything you see in SL is built using prims.

 

There are places in SL where you can get information to help you with building.

 

  The SJSU SLIS campus has building information. (55,86,33)

 

 

  SLs in-world building tutorial is located in the Ivory Tower of Prims.
